> We don't see the world as it is, we see it as we are. - Anaïs Nin

The first time I watched *Dead Poets Society*, I was in school. An early teen who had already worked his way through films from 90s and early 2000s. Films so well written that Facebook pages would quote them obsessively. 
I watched them, some very religiously, and when Robin Williams' quotes would surface on social media, I could identify them immediately.

The second time I watched, I was in college. A meek, distressed boy in his late teens. I got very emotional watching it, secretly shedding a tear or two. But there was a knot in my stomach. I was caught in a conflict between how I was trained to operate and how I felt while watching the movie. I viewed life through a very utilitarian lens, but alongside this, I was a sensitive person, in the sense of experiencing lots of emotions. But I wasn't allowing myself to feel their complete breadth nor the depth.

> No matter what anybody tells you, words and ideas can change the world. - John Keating 

What pulled me out was words. I am who I am today because of words written by people out there in the world. 
Hope. I learnt hope and optimism from those words, and to be energised about the future. You see, a utilitarian, outcome-focused world sucks the joy out of you, leaving little to no excitement about the uncertainty of life and what the future holds. 
Art and self-expression are so fundamental to being human. And these aren't things that have any "social utility", but a life devoid of them isn't an exciting one.

Roughly a lustrum back, I shared this very feeling with a friend, and I swear, I felt magic in that split second, saying out loud how I was looking forward to the life ahead, because I was hoping that it would be amazing. I was excited about the things out there, and I wanted to experience them all. And I did. And life did get better.

The third time I watched this movie was over a month back. And this time, I interpreted it in a completely different way. More than just sympathising with the characters, I could empathise. Life had happened, and I had grown to have a broader understanding of self and society — and most important of all, I had developed kindness for myself. And today, I feel very grateful, which is partly why I write as well. Someone's blog changed my entire life. The blog was the ripple that created the waves I am surfing today.

All of our emotions, and our ability to feel everything - the good, bad, ugly - makes us who we are. Art, music, poetry, stories, food, this is what enriches our lives. I hope to enrich my life even more as the clock turns and the calendars flip to the months and years ahead. I hope to write a lot as well. To live life with a full cup.

godspeed.
