title: How you do anything is how you do everything?
date: 2026-03-8
---
How you do anything is how you do everything. Sounds cool, right? If you are good at something, that would make you good at everything. Well I've been a long-time believer of this and I only realize now that my interpretation hasn't been correct. It's been an interpretation that was made while cruising at 30,000 feet.

This is not a universal law. How you take decisions and behave in one situation, in one particular context, is not generalizable. A friend of mine is very ambitious, loves what he's working on, and shows extreme attention to detail, however when it comes to cooking, oh my god, this person is the most lousy human being I've ever seen. How he does one thing is very clearly not how he does everything.

On the other hand, this principle does hold true in areas which are not working well. A person who avoids conflict will hold back on having uncomfortable conversations with their colleagues, and probably also not bring up what's bothering them in their personal relations as well.

So how do we do anything, how do we decide, and how do we behave? The decisions are driven by two things:
1. Values
2. Context

Values are part of your identity. Context is the situation. Decisions driven by your values will be consistent, but your situation will be constantly evolving, with way too many variables outside of your control.

What that means is that how you do anything doesn't really mean that's how you will do everything. If you're having a bad day, be kind to yourselves; it doesn't mean you'll mess up everything. If you're having a good streak, celebrate, but keep yourselves grounded. Maybe it was a series of good decisions, maybe it was a lot of luck in your favor, or a combination of both. You need to respect that.
