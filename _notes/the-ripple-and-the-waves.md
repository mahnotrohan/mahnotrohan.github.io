> "We don't see the world as it is, we see it as we are." 
> — Anaïs Nin

The first time I watched The Dead Poets Society, I was in school. An early teen who had already worked his way through films most of his peers hadn't touched yet. I watched them, I absorbed them, and when Robin Williams' quotes would surface on social media, I could place them instantly. 

The second time I watched, I was in college. A meek distressed boy, in late teens. I got very emotional watching it, secretly shedding a tear or two. The movie touched something in me. But there was a knot in my gut.

The mindset I was in, restricted me to view humans from a utilitarian lens, and I was in conflict with this. Because along with this mindset, I am a sensitive person. Not like a wallflower, but in the sense of experiencing lots of emotions. But I wasn't allowing myself to feel their complete breadth. A part of this was what led to migraines, and prolonged splitting headaches.

**Words can change the world**

What pulled me out was words. I am who I am today because of words written by people on the internet. I learnt from the words written by people to be optimistic and energised about future.
You see, a utilitarian, outcome focused world, sucks the joy out of you. With little to no excitement about the uncertainty of life, and what the future holds. Art, self expression, are so fundamental to being human. And these aren't things that have any "social utility" but a life devoid of them, isn't an exciting one.

Roughly, a decade back, in a cafe in CP, I told a friend this very feeling, and I swear, I felt magic in that split second, about how, I am looking forward to life ahead, because I am hoping that it will be amazing. I am excited about things out there, and I want to experience it all.
And I did. And life did get better.

The third time I watched this movie, was a month back. And this time, I interpreted the movie in a completely different way. More than just sympathising with the characters, I could empathise. 

Life had happened, and I had grown to have a broader understanding of self, the society, and most important of all, developed kindness, for self.
And today, I feel very grateful, and partly why I write as well. Someone's blog changed my entire life. The blog was the ripple, which created the waves, I am surfing today.

All of our emotions, and ability to feel - the good, bad, ugly, amazing - everything, makes us who we are. Art, music, poetry, stories, food, - this is what enriches our lives.

I hope to enrich my life even more, as the clock turns, and the calendars flip to months, and years ahead. I hope to write a lot as well. To live life, with a full cup.

godspeed.
