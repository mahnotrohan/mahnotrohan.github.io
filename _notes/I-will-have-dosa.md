I live with friends. We're all aware of our diets, and conscious about staying active.

One guy moved out and a new one moved in. He's a few years younger than us, good lad. And absolutely loaded with snacks.

We showed him the hall, and our hang out spot, and that's where he kept his stash of snacks. 

He didn't unpack any whey protein. No protein bars. Nothing. It was a little weird, because everyone I know, talks about protein at least once a day.

Well, when snacks are just there, we all tend to eat them. My resistance, is mostly a function of snacks being inaccessible. I've been here before, realising that my discipline was never really discipline. It was just the absence of opportunity

My former partner and I loved exploring food and ordering in. Between the two of us, she would pick out creative albeit sometimes indulgent and I was the one suggesting salads. I thought I was the sensible one. Looking back, she was more honest and truthful to herself. And she enjoyed her food in a way I rarely let myself.

There's a version of health consciousness that becomes its own kind of misery. Counting, calculating, negotiating every meal. The fat percentages you're chasing can start to cost more than it gives.

But the alternative isn't recklessness. Indulgence isn't the opposite of discipline. Sometimes it is the discipline, of listening to yourself, of not turning every meal into a moral test. With awareness about diet, listening to mind and body should also follow.

It's okay to have a bite of that pastry. You'll be fine. The next time someone asks if you want to split a cheesecake or go for dosas at midnight, if the stakes aren't high, just say yes. It'll be way more fun.
