- **Why is it important? & When is data no sufficient?**
    - Data tells you what is happening and by how much
    - But what is not happening, and why is it happening can be discovered only by talking to the users
    - You would have made assumptions on why something is happening, to build narrative around the quantitative insights
    - Testing these assumptions is important, and can be done only by talking to users
- **Attitude and behavior are different**
    - What people *say* (attitude) often diverges sharply from what they *do* (behavior)
    - Surveys, interview questions are **attitude research,** they capture the perceptions, beliefs, desires et.
    - User flow, funnels, adoption, retention indicate the **user behavior,** they capture, what people actually do
    - Neither is enough on it own, and the combination of both build clarity, why something is working or not working.
- Be prepared, with hypothesis to be tested
- Be specific
- Don’t seed question with assumptions when asking questions to the users
- Sometimes you have to set incentives to get people into a room to talk to them

### Notes from the Mom Test

- **The Three Rules**
    1. **Talk about their life, not your idea.** The moment you pitch, the conversation dies and people stop telling the truth and start managing your feelings.
    2. **Ask about specifics in the past, not generics or opinions about the future.** "Would you use this?" is worthless. "Walk me through the last time you dealt with this problem" is gold.
    3. **Talk less, listen more.** You're there to learn, not to sell or explain.
- How to ask good questions - replace hypotheticals with history, replace opinions with behavior, replace your idea with their workflow.
