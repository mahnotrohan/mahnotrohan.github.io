Latte Art

I started pulling shots at home a little over a year ago, mostly as an excuse to slow down in the mornings. Latte art came later — first as frustration, then as a small daily puzzle: milk temperature, pour height, the angle of the cup, the wrist.

These are my attempts, roughly organised by what I was trying to pour. The cleaner ones are at the top of each row; the honest misses are toward the bottom of the page. I kept the bad ones in on purpose — they're half the point.

Tulips & layered pours

The shape I've poured most. Stacking two or three lobes cleanly is still the hardest part.

Rosettas & leaf pours

Harder than they look. The side-to-side wiggle has to be metronomic, and the cup has to be the right shape.

Hearts

The first shape you learn. Deceptively hard to keep symmetrical.

Swans, ghosts & etchings

Done with a pick, not a pour. Slow, careful, closer to drawing.

Experiments & honest misses

Every skill has a landfill behind it. These are some of mine.
